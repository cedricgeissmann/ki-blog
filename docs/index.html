What is AI?

AI is the abbreviation for artificial intelligence. The word artificial intelligence is a compound word
that consist of the word artificial deriving from Latin artificiālis meaning “skill”, combined with the
Proto-Italic word inter-legere meaning to choose from something. But now that we have covered the
origin of the word let’s look at what exactly AI even is.

Artificial intelligence is often just referred to as AI, which is simply the abbreviation for artificial
intelligence. When talking about AI we refer to a rather wide-ranging branch of computer science
dealing with the development of intelligence machines that are able to perform tasks that under
normal circumstances would require the Intelligence of a human being. This means they can
perform many functions that normal computers are not able to perform on their own, since they
lack intelligence and must be led or guided by a human. This is not necessary with AI since it can
carry out Functions such as playing games, recognizing patterns, such as numbers, generating texts
or even images imitating certain artists, without requiring the involvement of a human being. These
machines are called AI since they seem to have an intelligence that allows them to execute functions
that require intelligence on their own. The fact that this is not fully the case yet will be covered in
the chapter Strong AI vs Weak AI.

A major characteristic of AI is that it learns by processing massive amounts of data. This process is
often supervised by humans, but in certain cases also autonomic, meaning the AI learns on its own.
This feature is especially important since it is a major advantage compared to other computers.

Strong AI Vs Weak AI

Since the term intelligence is rather difficult to define and the level of intelligence of certain AI
massively varies, AI is often categorized into so called strong and weak AI. But what exactly is
the difference between those two?

Strong AI or artificial general intelligence as it is often referred to has cognitive abilities that are
equivalent or even superior to the ones of an average human brain. This makes them, at least in
theory, equal, if not more intelligent than humans. This high level of intelligence or rather cognitive
abilities allow strong AI to carry out functions and solve problems that it has not been trained for.
This is also a main factor that is used to define intelligence, the ability to solve new or unknown
problems. However, including this in the definition of intelligence, none of the already existing AI
would be considered intelligent. The kind of AI that fulfills this definition is only the one we see in
science fiction movies such as Stars Wars or Star Trek. A concrete example of an AI in a science fiction
movie would be “The Doctor” from Star Trek: “Voyager”, which portrays an AI doctor in form of a
hologram. However, this type of AI does not actually exist yet. Therefor when applying those
standards to existing AI, none of them would be considered a strong AI, but rather a weak AI.

In contrast to strong AI, Weak AI exists already and has become a important part of our daily life,
even though it is strongly limited. Also, it is not actually intelligent but rather simulates human
intelligence. It can therefore only be used to fulfill a small range of functions. An example of
Weak AI would be Siri or Alexa.

How does AI work?

AI consists of a neural network consisting of neurons so called nodes that are arranged in layers.
There are generally three different types of layers: an Input Layer, Hidden Layers, and an Output Layer.
When data is fed intothe systems it starts at the Input Layer. There each node is connected to all the nodes
in the following Hidden Layer. However, the different connections between the nodes of the two different
layers have different weights. The weight of a connection determines the importance of it. Therefore,
even though all nodes of the first Hidden Layer receive the sameinformation / data, the weight of
the information that arrives is different in any node. To prevent an overflow of semi-important
information each of the nodes in the Hidden Layer performs an activation function. This function
sets a hurdle,meaning that the function controls the weight of the gathered information and if
the sum of the weight of all arriving information is too low it is not passed on.

Also, each and every node has a different so-called feature that it focuses on. Those features are not
given, instead they develop over time. The node is then in the information it is given looking for that
feature. Taking the number recognition system that we tested with the number 9 as an example: One node
in the Hidden Layer might focus on the circle on top, whereas a neighboring node might focus on the
stroke downwards. Those nodes are then only activated when the feature they focus on is present.

Dependent on the activation function used it might have a set value or border. In this case it only
activates if the weight of information is above a limit, otherwise the function can also be linear.In the
End, after the data was passed on from the Input Layer to the Hidden Layers it is eventually passed onto the
Output Layer. In the Output Layer all the information is gathered and put together into one single output.

How does AI learn?

In order to learn the AI is trained. In some cases, this training in supervised in others it is not
supervised by humans. This creates the risk of the AI learning offensive, racist or sexist language if
it’s learning is based on data from the Internet.But how exactly does it work?

In the beginning the weights of the connections between the nodes are assigned randomly. Then the data
passes through the entire system and an output is created. In a training situation the AI is then given a
reference. It is showed what the output is supposed to look like. The AI then compares its output to the
reference and adjusts the weights of the connections. The data is then fed into the system again and the
output again compared to a reference. This process is repeated over and over again. The rate at which
the AI adjusts its weights is called the learning rate. This learning rate is usually set somewhere
above 0 and below 1. Even though a high rate might seem optimal it is not. Having a learning rate of
1 would mean that the weight is perfectly adjusted to the reference. This would lead to the AI only
being trained to the last data that was given as a reference. In order to work correctly the
adjustments, have to be made slowly and gradually. The picture below illustrates the

Really interesting is AI however as soon as it has been trained to different data sets. This allows it
to analyze the given data set and to find patterns and regularities. Based on those patterns the AI then
adjusts and chooses an optimal distribution of the weight, so that each connection can be assigned to the
most optimal weight according to the data set given. Based on the data set the AI can then predict the
optimal weight distribution.

What influence does data have on AI?

The influence of data on AI is crucial. Data is the basis for the learning process of AI. Huge amounts of
data must be fed into an AI to properly train it. Also, the quality and diversity of the provided data have
a major impact on the performance and accuracy of AI since AI relies on data to train its algorithm.
Therefor AI is often supervised when learning because some data that the AI might find on the
internet is potentially harmful, racist, exist or simply of bad quality, sometimes even
containing false information. This shows us that data is not only “the life blood” of AI,
but also largely responsible for its quality and efficiency.

How does ChatGPT work?

ChatGPT is an AI programmed by OpenAI. ChatGPT can hold conversations and generate text, however it
knowledge of recent events is strongly limited and it cannot yet process input in form of pictures or
diagrams. ChatGPT attempts to understand your question or input and will then generate a string of words
as an output that predicts will answer your question best. It predicts those answers based on the data
it has been trained on. ChatGPT was also trained through a enormous amount of data. Roughly 5 billion
tokens to be exact. Tokens sequences of characters that frequently occur in texts. Whereas the AI
understands the statistical relationship between the tokens it is not aware of their meaning. Also,
Tokens are not necessarily words but simply parts of words. Since ChatGPT is not aware of the
meaning of the words it is generating it first generates a random sequence of tokens. Then it
compares those tokens with the input it was given and adjusts. This process is repeated
several times in the hope of eventually creating an output that matches the given input
and answers the question.

What are the limitations of ChatGPT & what to look out for?

When working with AI such as ChatGPT it is important to be aware of the limitations of the
AI used, as well as potential risks when using it. Since as with every other AI, also ChatGPT
has its limitations. Therefore, here are some factors and bullet points to look for when using it.

The most prominent issue with ChatGPT is probably that ChatGPT uses the Internet as a source and can
therefore not only spread fake news, but also develop a racist or sexist vocabulary. Another problem
is that ChatGPT cannot provide the sources used to generate these texts, making it difficult to prove
that the AI is trained where certain knowledge comes from. This is the reason why it should not be used
for Projects that require the listing of sources. Also, some cases have been reported where ChatGPT
has integrated fake news or invented facts, even about certain books or stories. This shows us that
checking the information given by ChatGPT is a must, to use it as a reliable source. Further it is
remarkable that even the website itself talks about limitations e.g.mentions the generation of
incorrect information that can occur.

A different common issue that is addressed as well is that I may produce harmful content or instruction
and that it has a limited knowledge of events after 2021. It is therefore advisable to only use ChatGPT
as a tool and to back up your data when using it. Also, ChatGPT shouldn’t be doing important work
without being supervised by a human being that can correct mistakes or back up certain facts.
As a matter of facts ChatGPT should not be working for you, but with you, supporting your work
as a tool and not replacing you.
