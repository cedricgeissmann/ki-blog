Artificial intelligence

What's AI and how does it work?

Artificial intelligence as a part of our modern society has become part of our lives. Although we all encounter AI applications on a daily basis in our web browsers or the commonly known AI applications such as chatgpt, kaiber or clipdrop, artificial intelligence is also involved in many other hidden digital processes that we as normal humans do not see, but which are of enormous significance.

Artificial intelligence has therefore become an essential part of our digitalised world and continues to demonstrate the importance of its place. For example face recognition in smartphones, human robots or automated driving, which is often used in modern Tesla models, would not be possible without AI. But what is AI actually?

In general, we can say that artificial intelligence, as a dynamic branch of computer science, deals with the question of creating mechanisms with which computers and digital devices can develop intelligent behaviour that is directly oriented towards the modern human example. Today's artificial intelligence enables learning, understanding and related action through hyperintelligent algorithms.

Overall AI's require a massive amount of data, which is based on the quantity and quality of the data, influences the end result. To arrive at the desired result, the technology extracts certain features from the data set and classifies them. In terms of the function of modern artificial intelligence, we differentiate between two types of artificial learning, machine learning and deep learning. The big difference between these two forms of learning in modern AI is that machine learning still needs human intervention so that the machine finally knows what to do, and deep learning is where the machine learns on its own, extracting and making sense of the data and information by itself.

Autonomous driving now serves as a common example for the described functioning of AI. The vehicle receives qualitative data from cameras, radar systems and other sensors, which provide a broad database of what is happening around the vehicle. In addition to receiving new data, the machine also evaluates its current driving behavior. With the help of its intelligence, the vehicle is ultimately able to rearrange the data it receives and decide whether to intervene. Ultimately, the vehicle receives feedback on how it can best master and manage the current traffic situation. The blog will go into these aspects in more detail later on with the involvement of the external AI application and use this example to show the importance of neural networks and their significance for artificial intelligence.

As a result, billions of US dollars are invested every year in dominant startups from a wide range of industries and experts are sure that around two-thirds of our technologies will be related to artificial intelligence. Neural network

How does a neural network work?

In order to understand the meaning of neural networks and the general automated learning of AI modelled on humans, we now need to take another step back and look at the question of the realisation of AI. In general, it must be seen that artificial intelligence can always be realised through the traditional way of programming, since a complex network of knowledge can be built up by stringing together if, then statements and other programmable control procedures. Such so-called "expert systems" are now often seen in modern medicine, where they are used to diagnose diseases through a variety of clinical pictures and symptoms. The problem with this type of realisation is that, depending on the complexity of the application, the algorithms used can sometimes become very complicated.

If you go one step further and take a closer look at machine learning, it is characterized by the fact that it learns independently and acquires the necessary rules by automated training of datasets. To get to the point, you have to admit that machine learning is the most popular method for realizing artificial intelligence. In the course of the blog, however, we will go back explicitly to this central point and now take a look at the deep learning and neural networks.

The so-called deep learning covers a unique and modern form of machine learning, which is a milestone of artificial intelligence and is an essential part of our modern technology. In this form, neural networks ANN, which amounts to programmatic replicas of human networked nerve cells, so-called neurons, are trained with data and the final results are compared with it. When comparing these results and the general deep learning, the nerve connections of these neurons are mathematically adjusted and re-weighted, respectively strengthened. The concept of deep learning derives from the point that there are many hidden levels between the initial input and the final output, which have a high complexity and determine this kind of realization of artificial intelligence. So if we take a closer look at the following Application, which has been set up in the context of this blog, it quickly becomes clear that this number recognition is based exactly on this deep learning and is built on a neural network, that is equipped with a corresponding dataset. The application is initially trained and the results are then compared with the originally searched digit, which thus leads to the adjustment of the nerve connections of the individual neurons and makes the digit recognition with each training passage a bit more precise. Machine learning

What's actually machine learning?

As already announced in the section on neural networks and deep learning, we will now return to machine learning and note its importance in relation to artificial intelligence. In general, it can be said that artificial intelligence has made significant progress in machine learning in recent years, which continues up to today and is characterised by the increasing availability of huge amounts of data and extremely high computing power, which is the basis for complex machine learning.

As already mentioned, machine learning represents the independent and autonomous learning process of a functioning algorithm through the constant repetition of a desired process until it is appropriately trained. The learning machine is oriented towards the criterion of goods and the information content of the given data. The big difference to conventional algorithms is, as already mentioned several times, that no solution path is modelled accordingly, but the machine develops its own ways of recognising and classifying data through machine learning.

A good example for the whole topic would be the many different robots that are currently being developed for various services, such as nursing or army service. If, for example, such a robot has the task of transporting a box of ammunition from A to B, it will find its own way to transport the essential ammunition by repeatedly trying out and adapting its task-based algorithm. The way in which it accesses the ammunition or the speed with which it walks will become clear in the course of its learning process and the human being does not need to make any further interventions. Influence of data

How do data influence artificial intelligence?

Let's take a closer look at the influence of data on modern artificial intelligence. Basically, it can be said that data plays an extremely important role in the development and use of artificial intelligence and serves as the basis of machine learning, which we have looked at before. However, in order to understand the general influence of data, certain points must be considered. In order to train an artificial intelligence based on its machine learning process, large amounts of data are required, which determines the precision and accuracy of the subsequent AI based on the points of view of the data representation and the general data volume. In this regard, data preprocessing is helpful, since the data used should first be pre-processed and cleaned to ensure the necessary data quality and to avoid data errors that restrict the AI. Data preprocessing includes some important steps, such as data cleaning, data normalization, and outlier removal, and feature engineering.In order to review the data quality and the general data representation, it must be said that the data used should always be complete, current and specifically representative for the respective application area. In order to additionally prevent this, data should be updated and adapted over time to maintain the performance of artificial intelligence and adapt it to various changes of the acute time.

At the end of this important topic, however, something should also be said about data protection and data ethics, as it is a very important point that is often underestimated. It is crucial to ensure that data is treated ethically and in privacy grounds in order to avoid potential bias and discrimination. In general, the importance of data and its impact on artificial intelligence should now be clear and we can move forward in the blog.

How does ChatGPT work?

The popular and well-known ChatGPT is a chatbot based on artificial intelligence from the global company OpenAI, which was co-founded by Elon Musk and has gained massive interest in recent years. The chatbot usually communicates with users in various languages such as German and English and evaluates questions with detailed answers. But in general, the question arises how this ChatGPT works and is built up.

To address the question of how ChatGPT works at all and how this application could become so popular, you first have to look at how the artificial intelligence behind the chatbot was trained. ChatGPT was trained with a specific methodology called Reinforcement Learning from human feedback (RLHF) that has shaped the chatbot so significantly. What are the limitations of the AI ChatGPT and what to look for in the use of it.

With regard to artificial intelligence and the chatbot ChatGPT, we should finally get to know the limits of this AI and record on what you have to look at when using it. In general, it can be said that the debate about ChatGPT has increased drastically in recent months and has caused significant cuts in the field of education. Thus, the DFKI Labor Berlin warns against bringing ChatGPT unreflected and free to the Andwenung when it comes to the generation of specialized text. Such warnings and the general reference to conventional metodes is based exactly on the limits of AI, which we are now discussing.

The texts and answers generated by chatgpt often seem very plausible and true, which exactly shows one of the essential problems, as these can be simple plagiarism from the training data and also contain some serious errors and mistakes, which are simply not correct. Thus, according to the DFKI Labor Berlin, a recently published perprint points out that people can use digital tools such as ChatGPT, or other artificial intelligence to produce desired code, which contains many security gaps, although users expect to have generated fewer vulnerabilities related to this AI. In general, this ignorance is a big problem and limits the use of the chatbot chatGPT considerably. ChatGPT is also very limited in terms of source work in relation to generated texts, as sources are sometimes either incorrectly specified or simply invented by the AI, that do not exist at all and violate so many basic norms in relation to written texts. The limits of chatbots such as ChatGPT and other artificial intelligence-based applications are therefore numerous and in addition to the points discussed, still amount to many other aspects of the limitation, which one must simply be aware of as a user of ChatGPT.
