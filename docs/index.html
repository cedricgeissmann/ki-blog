Artificial intelligence

What's AI and how does it work?

Artificial intelligence as a part of our modern society has become part of our lives. Although we all encounter AI applications on a daily basis in our web browsers or the commonly known AI applications such as chatgpt, kaiber or clipdrop, artificial intelligence is also involved in many other hidden digital processes that we as normal humans do not see, but which are of enormous significance.

Artificial intelligence has therefore become an essential part of our digitalised world and continues to demonstrate the importance of its place. For example face recognition in smartphones, human robots or automated driving, which is often used in modern Tesla models, would not be possible without AI. But what is AI actually?

In general, we can say that artificial intelligence, as a dynamic branch of computer science, deals with the question of creating mechanisms with which computers and digital devices can develop intelligent behaviour that is directly oriented towards the modern human example. Today's artificial intelligence enables learning, understanding and related action through hyperintelligent algorithms.

Overall AI's require a massive amount of data, which is based on the quantity and quality of the data, influences the end result. To arrive at the desired result, the technology extracts certain features from the data set and classifies them. In terms of the function of modern artificial intelligence, we differentiate between two types of artificial learning, machine learning and deep learning. The big difference between these two forms of learning in modern AI is that machine learning still needs human intervention so that the machine finally knows what to do, and deep learning is where the machine learns on its own, extracting and making sense of the data and information by itself.

Autonomous driving now serves as a common example for the described functioning of AI. The vehicle receives qualitative data from cameras, radar systems and other sensors, which provide a broad database of what is happening around the vehicle. In addition to receiving new data, the machine also evaluates its current driving behavior. With the help of its intelligence, the vehicle is ultimately able to rearrange the data it receives and decide whether to intervene. Ultimately, the vehicle receives feedback on how it can best master and manage the current traffic situation. The blog will go into these aspects in more detail later on with the involvement of the external AI application and use this example to show the importance of neural networks and their significance for artificial intelligence.

With regard to artificial intelligence, it is important to know that AI is not just an idea from yesterday, but has been gaining massive momentum since the 1950s, which continues to this day and has seen a massive increase in interest in recent years. As a result, billions of US dollars are invested every year in dominant startups from a wide range of industries and experts are sure that around two-thirds of our technologies will be related to artificial intelligence. Megatrend

According to PWC, AI will increase global gross domestic product by 14 percent and contribute $15.7 trillion by 2030. According to the report, AI is a "business advantage" for 72 percent of all business leaders. Milestones

In 1997, the computer Deep Blue triumphed in chess against world champion Garry Kasparov. It was the breakthrough for artificial intelligence in duels with human opponents. Other significant victories followed: for example, in Scrabble and even the TV show Jeopardy! The final success was achieved by AI in what experts consider the most complicated board game: Go. In 2016, the AlphaGo computer defeated one of the world's best Go players.

The future of work

Experts believe that the fear of millions of jobs being eliminated by artificial intelligence is unfounded. Less than five percent of jobs are defined in such a way that they can be completely automated, says Michael Chui, a partner in the research department at McKinsey, for example. However, the focus is shifting for many activities, he adds. "There are things that machines are not as good at, such as interacting with other people and creative work," Chui says.
