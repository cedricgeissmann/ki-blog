Process

Digit learning and recognition is a part of machine learning which refers to identifying a certain, in this case, handwritten digit (from 0-9). It contains the following steps:

1.) Dataset: The first step is to gather the dataset of handwritten digits, which consists of those digits and their corresponding labels (0-9)

2.) Differentiate digits: In the second step the system learns to differentiates digits, often based on its pixel intensity, the placement of pixels, edges and contours.

3.) Training: During training the model learns to use its knowledge of differentiation and the dataset to match each written number to the corresponding digit.

4.) Evaluation: The prediction of the model are compared to ground truth labels to measure its accuracy. If there are errors while predicting or a low accuracy, then the model will refine itself and continue training.

Usage

This process has a wide variety of usage:

  • Postal system: Postal systems use this process to recognize mails and can sort them a lot quicker without humankind needing to seperatly sort them.

  • Finance and Banking: Digit recognition is utilized in banking and finance for tasks such as recognizing handwritten checks and reading account numbers on checks or deposit slips.

  • Education: Digit recognition technology can be used in educational settings to assess students' handwritten responses to numerical questions or quizzes. It allows for automated grading and provides instant feedback to students.

    • Problems

    Problem, which may occur within digit recognition may be:

  • Variety of handwriting: Because everyone has different handwriting styles, the model may not be able to recognize certain handwritings. Especially when people write numbers in another way. Eg. some people write the number 1 consisting of one line while others write it with an additional small line towards the left.

  • Overlapping: Sometimes digits may overlap, leading to the system being confused about this ambiguous case and therefore giving an inaccurate result.

  • Limited Training Data: If there is a limit of data, the system won't have enough knowledge and will have a hardtime to train its accuracy.
